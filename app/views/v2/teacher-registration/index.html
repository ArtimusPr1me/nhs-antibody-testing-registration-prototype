You can now sign up for a coronavirus (COVID-19) home antibody test if you're a teacher or teaching support staff.

Check you're eligible

You can sign up for an antibody test if:

  • you're a teacher or teaching support staff
  • you've got a mobile phone
  • you're aged 18 or over

It does not matter if you've had the virus before or not.

About the test

The coronavirus antibody test is a fingerprick blood test that you do at home (it is sent to you in the post).

The home test kit comes with everything you need and simple step-by-step instructions. There's a video to help you.

You take the test and return your sample in the post. You'll get your results a few days later.

If you want to know more, watch this video

Why take the test

The NHS needs to learn more about who has already had the virus and how it is spreading. Many people may have had mild symptoms or none at all.

Find out more antibody testing

The test checks for coronavirus antibodies. If you have them, it is likely you've had the virus before.

This is not an immunity test. It cannot tell you when you had the virus or how effective (if at all) any antibodies are. COVID-19 is a new virus and doctors do not yet know if it is possible for you to catch it again or spread it.

How to sign up

You'll be asked a few quick questions to check you're eligible and for some basic information about you, like your contact details, gender, and if you've had the virus before. It takes 5 minutes.

After you've signed up

You'll get a text inviting you to order a home test kit when once becomes available. But this will not be straight away because stocks are limited and need to be sent to certain people first.

Most people will receive their invite within a few weeks, but it can take up to 3 months.

! Warning Only teachers and teaching staff can be tested at the moment. The number of tests is limited, so there may be a wait before you get invited.
