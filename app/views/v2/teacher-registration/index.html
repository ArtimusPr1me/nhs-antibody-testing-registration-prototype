You may be able to take part in a national research study into coronavirus (COVID-19) if you're a teacher or teaching support staff. Use this service to check you're eligible and sign up to take part.

Aim of the antibody research

The NHS and medical scientists need to learn more about who has already had the virus and how it is spreading in the UK at a national, regional and local level. Many people may have had mild symptoms or none at all.

Collecting this data will help the NHS and the UK plan its response to the spread of the virus.

Find out more about the research

What do you have to do to take part

To take part in the research you have to take a coronavirus antibody test. It's a fingerprick blood test that you do at home (it is sent to you in the post).

The home test kit is safe, accurate and easy to use. It comes with everything you need and simple step-by-step instructions. There's a video to help you.

You take the test and return your sample in the post. You'll get your results a few days later.

If you want to know more, watch this video and read more detail.

Antibody test

The test checks for coronavirus antibodies. If you have them, it is likely you've had the virus before.

This is not an immunity test. It cannot tell you when you had the virus or how effective (if at all) any antibodies are. COVID-19 is a new virus and doctors do not yet know if it is possible for you to catch it again or spread it.

Find out more about antibody testing and what your result means.

Who can take part in the research

You can take part in the research study if:

  • you're a teacher or teaching support staff
  • you've got a mobile phone
  • you're aged 18 or over

It does not matter if you've had the virus before or not.

Find out more about who counts a teaching or teaching support staff.

How take part in the research study

There are 4 steps.

Step 1 - check you're comfortable doing a fingerprick test

Step 2 - sign up to take part in the research
You'll be asked a few quick questions to check you're eligible and for some basic information about you, like your contact details, gender, and if you've had the virus before.

Step 3 - wait to be invited to order a test
You'll get a text inviting you to order a test kit and take part in the research. It may not happen straight away.

Step 4 - take the test
Take the test at home following the step by step instructions. You'll be emailed a copy of your results.

! Warning Only teachers and teaching staff can sign up to take part at the moment.
