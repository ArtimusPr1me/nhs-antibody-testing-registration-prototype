If you've been invited to take part in the national coronavirus research study you can use this service to order an antibody home test kit.

The research

The research will help the NHS and medical scientists learn more about who has already had the virus and how it is spreading in the UK at a national, regional and local level.

Find out why the research is needed
Check if you can take part in the research

The antibody home test kit

This is a fingerprick blood test that you do at home. It checks for coronavirus antibodies. If you have them, it is likely you have had the virus before.

The test is free and:

  • safe, accurate and easy to use
  • approved by Public Health England
  • takes about X days to arrive

To support the research: take the test and return your sample in the post. You'll get your results a few days later.

Who can order a test kit

You must be invited to take part in the research before you can order a test kit.

Check if you can take part in the research

How to order your test kit

Step 1 - make sure you have an invite code
Invite codes are 16 digits long and sent by text from the Coronavirus antibody testing service. You will only have one if you've been invited to take part in the research.

Step 2 - confirm your details
You'll be asked to confirm contact details and some information about you to help inform the research and help us

Step 3 - order your test kit
Provide your delivery address and place your order.
You'll get your test kit in the post X days later.

! Please note You can only order a test kit if you have been invited to take part in the research.
