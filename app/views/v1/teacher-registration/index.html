Ask for a home fingerprick blood test to help support an NHS research project. The NHS wants to understand who has had the virus before, and how it is spreading in the UK.

About the test

The test checks for coronavirus antibodies. If you have them, it's likely you've had the virus before.

The test cannot tell you when you had it or how effective your antibodies are.

If you've had the virus before, you must still follow the same rules as everyone else to lower the risk of catching it again and spreading it.

What the coronavirus antibody test results mean

! Warning This is not a swab test to check if you have coronavirus now

Who can ask for a test

This fingerprick blood test is only available for teachers and teaching assistants at the moment.

Your testing options

There are 2 ways to get tested:

  • use an antibody home test kit
  • go to an antibody test site

You should get your results within 24 hours for home tests or 2 days if you use a test site.

Registration takes a few minutes.
